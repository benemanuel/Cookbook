# Biscotti Mandelbrot

## Ingredients

## Instructions

juliusroberts


Cantucci or Biscotti are a staple at home each Christmas. I make them every year as edible presents and they always go down a treat. If you can track down some Vin Santo to dip them in you have one of my favourite puddings on the planet, but they're equally great dipped in a strong coffee too. Here is the recipe with chocolate, hazelnuts and orange zest, play around with the nuts and flavourings, I particularly love them with pistachios.


250g plain flour


240g caster sugar


2 large eggs or 3 small ones


2 oranges zested


2tsp of fennel seeds


200g hazelnuts (almonds or pistachios)


31\.8K


150g dark chocolate


1 tsp baking powder


Icing sugar


A pinch of salt


Toast the nuts at 200°c until gently golden brown, then crush and chop into smaller pieces.


Bash the fennel seeds with a bit of the sugar in a pestle and mortar then chop up the chocolate. Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix thoroughly, then split into two and roll out into logs on a bed of icing sugar to stop them sticking. Lay on baking trays with parchment paper and bake at 200°c degrees for 20 minutes. Then remove, leave to cool a little, then chop into biscuits at an angle while still warm. Lay out on the trays and bake again, but now at 150°c for another 20 minutes or more, turning half way through until they are dry, but not too hard. Store in a jar when cooled and they'll last ages! Not that they ever stick around too long.
