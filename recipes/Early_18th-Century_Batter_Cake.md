# Early 18th-Century Batter Cake

## Ingredients

## Instructions

**Ingredients**

* **Flour**: 237\.5 g
* **Dark raisins or sultanas:** 142\.5 g
* **Melted butter**: 83 g
* **Mature stiff sourdough starter (60% hydration)**: 100 g
  + (Made from 62\.5 g flour \+ 37\.5 g water)
* **Egg yolks**: 2 large
* **38% Cream**: 100 ml
* **Candied citron, lemon, and orange peel**: 35\.5 g
* **Sack (sherry or sweet cooking wine)**: 25 ml
* **Sugar**: 24 g
* **Ground mace**: 1\.5 g
* **Ground clove**: 1 g
* **Grated nutmeg**: 1\.5 g
* **Beaten cinnamon**: pinch (to taste)
* **Honey water**: 2 tbls

---


#### **Instructions**

1. *(Optional but recommended)* Soak raisins and candied peel in the sack for 15–30 minutes to enhance flavor and texture.
2. Best flavor twist instead of raisins: A mix of raisins \+ dried cherries (half\-half) adds depth while maintaining balance.
3. In a large bowl, mix the flour (237\.5 g), sugar, spices, and soaked fruits.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, cream, egg yolks, and honey water.
5. Add the wet mixture and the 100 g mature stiff sourdough starter to the dry ingredients. Stir until a thick, cohesive batter forms. If it feels overly stiff, adjust with a small amount of cream or water.
6. Cover and let rise in a warm place for 3–5 hours, or until visibly puffed.
7. Stir down or gently fold the batter to redistribute air pockets and fruit.
8. Line the bottom of a 6\-inch (15 cm) baking hoop or springform pan with parchment paper. Grease sides if needed.
9. Pour in the batter and smooth the surface.
10. Bake at **180°C (350°F)** for **40–50 minutes**, or until a skewer inserted in the center comes out clean.
11. Let cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then remove and cool completely on a wire rack.
