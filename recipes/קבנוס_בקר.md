## Ingredients

* Beef (30% fat content)
* 14% Nitrite salt
* 0\.6% Erythorbate
* 4g Black pepper
* 2g Nutmeg
* 1g Cayenne pepper
* Lamb or collagen casings (23mm diameter)

## Instructions

1. Stuffing: Stuff the beef mixture into lamb or collagen casings with a diameter of 23mm.
2. Smoking: Smoke the sausages at 72 degrees Celsius for 4\-6 hours.
3. Cooling: Cool the sausages thoroughly.


## Notes:

* Nitrite salt: This is a curing salt that gives the sausage its characteristic pink color and helps prevent the growth of harmful bacteria.
* Erythorbate: This is an antioxidant that helps enhance the color of the meat and improve the curing process.
* Spices: The black pepper, nutmeg, and cayenne pepper provide flavor. You can adjust the amounts to your taste.
* Casings: Lamb casings are natural and give the sausage a traditional look. Collagen casings are a synthetic alternative that is more consistent in size.


### Additional Tips:

* Meat Grinder: You'll need a meat grinder to process the beef and mix it with the seasonings.
* Stuffer: A sausage stuffer will make it easier to fill the casings.
* Smoking Equipment: You can use a smoker, grill, or even a specialized sausage smoker.
* Temperature Control: Maintaining a consistent temperature during the smoking process is crucial for achieving the desired results.
* Cooling: Ensure the sausages cool completely before storing or serving.
