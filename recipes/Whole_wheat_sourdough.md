# Whole wheat sourdough

## Ingredients

## Instructions

I loosely used bread code’s whole wheat sourdough recipe:


First loaf (first pic):


\- 400g King Arthur ww flour

\- 340g water (did an autolyse test and 85% had the best gluten development)

\- 80g rye starter (more than doubled in 9 hrs at 1:5:5\)

\- 8g salt


1\. Stirred together for 5 mins, let rest covered for 10, slap n fold for 10 mins on counter, rested for 5 mins.

2\. Laminated, tightened, and rested covered.

3\. Coil folds at 3hrs, 5hrs, and 8hrs

4\. At 8hrs the tester I extracted was doubled so I shaped it and fridged it overnight.

5\. Preheated pizza stone for 1 hr at 500F, then baked at 480 for 25 mins after spritzing with water (I don’t have a Dutch oven, I use a tray of water underneath the stone and an upside\-down baking sheet above).

6\. Lowered heat to 450, took out “improv steam oven” and baked 15 minutes (in retrospect it was way too early, I’m used to white flour color so I jumped the gun).

7\. Let rest for 2 hrs before cutting.


My tasting notes of the first bake was that it was really freaking sour and the inside was too dense, almost dry. I realized 20% starter was maybe too much for the summer, maybe not enough steam, and maybe needed more resting before cutting.


Second loaf (pics 2 \& 3\):

\- the same as above but only 40g starter


1\. Mixed for 10 minutes (felt that helped build more dough strength), rested 10 mins, counter slap n fold for 10 mins, 5 minute rest

2\. Laminated and rested, covered.

3\. Coil folds @ 3 and 6hrs (definitely didn’t spread so easily as the first).

4\. At 9 hours, it wasn’t really spreading out and seemed puffy (I popped a few big surface bubbles) but the tester was only at 25% but I fridged it anyway in my baneton.

5\. Baked same as above, but added an ice cube at the beginning to the stone and a few more spritzes of water throughout.

6\. After taking out the steam catcher system, I baked for 25 mins but I think next time I’ll keep going even longer.

7\. Rested for 6 hrs


Much better flavor, milder but still tangy, more bouncy crumb but still quite dense in the middle though it was about 25% more open and moist than the first one.


\*\*my issues:\*\* I’m not sure what I can do to get a bit more oven spring and airier crumb with 100% ww, would adding just 10% or so bread flour make any difference? Also, I’m open to suggestions for any aesthetic improvements.
