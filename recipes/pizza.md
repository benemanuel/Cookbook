# Pizza

![](/images/pizza.jpg)

## Ingredients

### Dough for 3 Pizzas

* **500g** flour (Type 405)
* **250ml** water
* **21g** fresh yeast (half a cube)
* **10g** salt
* **25ml** olive oil

### Topping

* Whatever tastes good

---

## Preparation

### Dough

* Put all ingredients except for the flour into a bowl and stir lightly.
* Then add the flour and knead for about 5 minutes using a hand mixer (dough hooks).
* Afterwards, knead by hand for another 10 minutes.
* Let the dough rise in a bowl covered with a damp cloth for at least 30 minutes.
* Then divide into 3 equal pieces, knead briefly again, and shape into balls.
* Place these into three small bowls and let them rise again for at least 60 minutes.
* Finally, shape the balls into round pizza bases by hand (do not use a rolling pin!) and add toppings as desired.

### Topping

* The pizza can be topped however you like.
* It is worth using high-quality ingredients.
* Furthermore: less is more.
* Do not top too thickly!

### Baking

* The rule here is: the hotter, the better.
* In a preheated oven at 300°C convection and on a pizza stone, the pizza takes about 6-8 minutes.
* Neither the pizza stone nor the 300°C are absolutely necessary, but for the optimal result, the oven should be as hot as humanly possible.

---

## Tips

* For an especially good dough, the dough can be prepared the day before and rise in the refrigerator overnight.
* In this case, the amount of yeast should be halved.
* It is extremely important to knead the dough long enough.
* You must not be lazy here under any circumstances.
* Shaping the dough by hand requires some practice but is learned relatively quickly.
* It is worth it, as using a rolling pin would otherwise press all the air out of the dough.
* The pizza dough can be easily frozen.
* Simply shape it into a round form, spread with tomato sauce, and then freeze.
* Once the pizza is frozen, put it in a freezer bag to prevent freezer burn.
