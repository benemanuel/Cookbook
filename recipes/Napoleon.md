# Napoleon

## Ingredients

## Instructions

[SEARCH](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/przepisy/szukaj/szukaj-w-opisach)

![Cream cake / Napoleon](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/kremowka.jpg)

I present to you a recipe for a delicious fondant (napoleon). Many have been arguing about the name of this cake ;\-). I come from Lesser Poland and it will always be a cream cake for me. Napoleon is a tall cake with lots of pink or white foam inside.

I really wanted to bake a fondant similar to the one you can buy (at least in my regions) in pastry shops. It should be on puff pastry, postponed pudding mass (rather thick pudding) and sprinkled with powdered sugar from above. The dough is delicate, stratified wonderfully and grows well. I wanted mass to be a lot. To get the amount of mass that is in purchased cream cakes I needed to cook the pudding from 1 liter of milk. Despite this, the mass holds well and does not flow out.

### Ingredients:

**Puff pastry:**

* 300 g butter
* 300 g wheat flour
* 1 egg (size M)
* ½ tablespoons of vinegar (about 5%)
* approx. 100 ml hot water


**Pudding mass:**

* 1 liter of milk
* 50 g butter
* 4 yolks
* 2 eggs (size M)
* 200 g sugar
* 2 teaspoons [of vanilla sugar](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/przepisy/polprodukty-cukiernicze/377-cukier-waniliowy)
* 120 g wheat flour
* 70g potato starch


**Additionally:**

* powdered sugar for sprinkling

### A method of preparing:

1. Prepare puff pastry: 300g butter and 100g flour knead to a homogeneous mass. Put the mass on a piece of food foil, cover with a second piece of foil and flatten it by hand or roll out into a square about 2 cm thick. Put the butter mass wrapped in foil in the fridge.
2. During this time, knead the dough from the rest of the ingredients, adding enough water to make it look like a pierogi dough. Roll out the dough on a flour\-covered top into a circle.


![2](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/2.jpg)

1. Put the cooled butter mass from the fridge in the center of the cake. Fold the dough into an envelope and glue the edges thoroughly.


![3](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/3.jpg)

![4](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/4.jpg)

1. Invert the dough with the joints downwards and roll out into a long rectangle approximately 1 cm thick. (Always roll in one direction).


![5](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/5.jpg)

1. Fold the dough into three parts, like a letter. Lightly roll out (just press with a roller so that it is a little thinner, then it cools better) and wrap in foil. Place the wrapped dough on a wooden board and place in the freezer for 15 minutes.


![6](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/6.jpg)

![7](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/7.jpg)

1. Pull the dough out of the freezer, turn it 90 ° and roll it out again into an oblong rectangle. Re\-submit as a letter. Lightly roll out (just press with a roller to make the dough thinner), wrap in foil and put in the freezer for 15 minutes. Repeat this process two more times, each time putting the dough into the freezer for 15 minutes.
2. The finished dough should be cut in half. Wrap one half in foil and put in the fridge.
3. Roll out the other half of the dough onto a flour\-covered countertop, into a large rectangular cake. (The dough shrinks significantly under the influence of baking). Transfer the dough to a large, flat oven tray lined with baking paper, possibly even the edges with a knife and prick with a fork.


![8](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/8.jpg)

1. Bake in a preheated oven for about 15 minutes, until golden brown, at a temperature of 220 ° C, top\-down heater. After a few minutes of baking, the dough will rise. Open the oven and prick the bulge with a fork. Bake the second cake in the same way. Allow the pies to cool. One cake, intended for the top of the dough, cut (preferably with a knife and teeth) into squares of the size that are to be fondant. (This will make it easier to cut the dough later.)


![9](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/9.jpg)

1. Prepare the pudding mass: Approx. Boil 750 ml of milk with butter, sugar and vanilla sugar. Mix the rest of the milk with eggs, yolks, flour and starch. Add to boiling milk, mixing so that no lumps form. Cook a thick pudding. Leave the pudding to cool slightly, stirring briefly every now and then. Pour still slightly warm on the first puff cake. (It is best to wrap the bottom puff pastry with a strip made of aluminum foil rolled several times so that the pudding does not flow out.) Arrange the cut squares on the pudding mass.


![10](https://www.domowe-wypieki.pl/images/content/300/10.jpg)

1. Put the dough in the fridge for at least a few hours.
2. Sprinkle with powdered sugar. It is best to cut the dough with a sharp knife with cloves.
