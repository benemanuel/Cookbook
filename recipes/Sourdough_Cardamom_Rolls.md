# Sourdough Cardamom Rolls

## Sourdough Cardamom Rolls (Pasta Madre, Hybrid Rescue Version)

**Yield:** \~12 rolls  
**Total flour:** \~550g (including starter)  
**Fermentation temp target:** 24–27°C  
**Bake target internal temp:** 93–94°C

---

## Ingredients

### Dough

* 150g stiff starter (≈50% hydration, cold is fine)
* 400g bread flour
* 50g rustic / higher extraction flour
* 280g whole milk (room temp)
* 100g sugar
* 1 egg
* 100g soft butter
* 8g salt
* 3g freshly ground cardamom


### Filling

* 100g soft butter
* 100g sugar
* 4g freshly ground cardamom
* Pinch salt
* (Optional: orange zest)


### Optional Rescue (if fermentation weak)

* 3g instant yeast
* 20g warm milk (≈35°C)

---

## Instructions

## 1️⃣ Autolyse – 30 Minutes

Mix until no dry flour remains:

* Milk
* Starter (cut small)
* All flour


Rest 30 minutes.

---


## 2️⃣ Final Mix

Add:

* Egg
* Sugar
* Cardamom


Mix to combine.

Add:

* Salt


Knead 5 minutes.

Add butter gradually and knead until:

* Smooth
* Elastic
* Slightly tacky but not sticky


Total kneading: \~10–12 minutes.

---


## 3️⃣ Bulk Fermentation (22–23°C Room)

Let rise **5–7 hours** until:

* 60–70% rise
* Surface smoother
* Slight gas visible


Give one fold after 2–3 hours.

### If fermentation stalls:

Dissolve 3g yeast in 20g warm milk.
Knead gently into dough.
Ferment at 26–27°C until 60–70% risen.

---


## 4️⃣ Shape

1. Roll to rectangle (\~5mm thick).
2. Spread filling.
3. Fold in thirds (letter fold).
4. Roll slightly thinner.
5. Cut strips and twist into knots.
6. Place in pan with space.

---


## 5️⃣ Short Room Proof

Let sit 45–60 minutes at room temp  
until slight puffing begins.

---


## 6️⃣ Overnight Final Proof (Recommended)

Refrigerate 8–14 hours at 4°C.

Next day:
Let sit 1–2 hours at room temp  
until visibly puffy and airy.

---


## 7️⃣ Bake

* 190°C preheated oven
* 18–25 minutes depending on pan size


### Internal Temperature Target:

**93–94°C**

If tops darken early:

* Tent loosely with foil
* Reduce oven to 170–175°C
* Continue until 93–94°C

---


## Optional Syrup (Highly Recommended)

Boil:

* 100g sugar
* 100g water


Brush immediately after baking.

---


# Key Technical Notes

### Dough Temperature

* Ideal: 24–27°C
* Above 30°C: butter softens too much, structure weakens

---


### Windowpane Expectations

Enriched sourdough will:

* Stretch but tear slightly
* Improve more through fermentation than kneading


Perfect windowpane is not required.

---


### Signs Dough Is Ready to Shape

* 60–70% rise
* Slight dome
* Feels lighter
* Poke springs back slowly

---


### Why 93–94°C Internal?

Milk \+ egg \+ butter require higher set temperature.
Below 90°C \= slightly gummy crumb.

---


### Cardamom Balance (Freshly Ground)

* 3g in dough
* 4g in filling


Fresh cardamom is stronger than pre\-ground.

---


# Lessons for Next Time

* A 2\-week\-old stiff starter should be refreshed once before enriched dough.
* Enriched sourdough benefits from warmer fermentation (26°C ideal).
* Packed rolls in a round pan bake slower in the center.
