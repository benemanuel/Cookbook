# Oatmeal Creme Pies

<https://sallysbakingaddiction.com/homemade-little-debbie-oatmeal-creme-pies/#tasty-recipes-76598>  

or

<https://www.sugarandsoul.co/oatmeal-cream-pies/#recipe>  

or

Cookie Ingredients:

* 1 1/4 cups of butter (room temperature)

* 1 cup packed dark brown sugar

* 1/2 cup granulated sugar

* 1 large egg

* 2 tsp of vanilla extract

* 1 Tbsp of dark molasses

* 1 1/2 cups all purpose flour

* 1 tsp baking soda

* 1/2 tsp salt

* 3/4 tsp cinnamon

* 1/4 tsp ground cloves (or nutmeg and/or a pinch of pumpkin pie spice)

* 3 cups quick cooking oats

Filling Ingredients:

* 3/4 cups butter (room temperature)

* 3 cups of confectionery sugar (sifted)

* 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

* 3 Tbsp heavy cream

Tools:

* 2 boobs and a bra (optional)

* 2 large bowls

* 1 regular bowl

* Hand mixer

* Cookie sheet

* Cooling rack

## Ingredients

## Instructions

1. Take the butter out of the refrigerator the night before. If you forgot to then put the butter in your bra while you get everything organized.

2. In one bowl cream together the 1 1/4 cups room temp butter, 1 cup packed dark brown sugar, and 1/2 cup granulated sugar.

3. Whip for about a minute and a half until it gets fluffy and lighter in color.

4. Add one large egg, two tsp vanilla extract and 1 Tbsp dark molasses.

5. In the other bowl, sift together 1 1/2 cups all purpose flour, 1 tsp baking soda, 1/2 tsp salt, 3/4 tsp cinnamon, and 1/4 tsp ground cloves/nutmeg/pumpkin spice. Whisk to combine.

6. Add 3 cups quick cooking oats. Whisk to combine.

7. Add dry ingredients to the wet a little at a time. It’s best to mix by hand because the batter is thick and you don’t want to overwork the dough. Just lightly fold it over until lightly combined. For this recipe you do not need to chill, it needs to spread out. Form equal(ish) 3 ounce balls.

8. Bake at 375 degrees for 11 min.

9. Take out and leave on cookie sheet for 3 minutes before transfer to cooling rack.

10. For the filling, in a bowl put 3/4 cups room temp butter and 3 cups of sifted confectionery sugar and mix.

11. Add 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract. Mix that up a little.

12. Add up to 3 Tbsp heavy cream to make your preferred consistency. This part is up to you.

13. Once you're good with your consistency, whip for 4 minutes until fluffy.

14. Add a bit of salt to taste. Like a pinch or two.

15. Once cookies are cooled assemble them into sandwiches.
