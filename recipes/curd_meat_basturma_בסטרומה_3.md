## Instructions

To make a traditional curd meat basturma, you generally want to let the meat hang in the refrigerator to dry and cure for around 10 to 14 days before cold smoking. Here’s a more detailed timeline and tips for each phase:

1\. Dry Curing Phase (10\-14 Days)

Goal: Remove excess moisture and allow the flavors of the spices to penetrate the meat.

Process:

Hang the meat in the refrigerator (ideally on a wire rack to allow airflow) at temperatures between 34\-40°F (1\-4°C).

Check for firmness, which indicates it’s drying well.

Duration: 10 to 14 days, or until the surface is dry, and the meat feels firmer to the touch.

2\. Cold Smoking Phase

Timing: Once the meat has fully cured and dried in the refrigerator, cold smoke it at around 70\-90°F (21\-32°C) for 12\-24 hours depending on the smokiness you prefer.

Keep the smoking chamber's temperature low to avoid cooking the meat while infusing it with smoke.

Tips

Air Circulation: Ensure the meat hangs freely with good air circulation on all sides.

Weight Monitoring: Weighing the meat before and after curing can help you check for moisture loss (ideal is about a 30% weight reduction).

Patience for Flavor: The longer the curing phase, the more intense the flavor.
