## Instructions

My personal experience with paneer and I almost always end up making good paneer :)

* Add lemon juice (I prefer vinegar though for faster curdling) spoon by spoon. Pour one teaspoon, stir for a minute, then add next spoonful.
* Do not let the milk boil or overflow. I use a food thermometer, best temp for curdling is around 80–85 degree C.
* Stop adding lemon or vinegar once it has started curdling well.
* I only use a full fat milk. Of course results were better when I used buffalo milk (which I get very rarely though).
* My thumbrule is max. 2 tbsp of lemon juice for 1 liter of milk.
* Use a cheesecloth. It does give better results.
* Control fire and control acidic agent, you will have good paneer. Acidic agent is often the culprit for grainy paneer.
* Another thumbrule, I keep around 10 kg weight on top paneer.
