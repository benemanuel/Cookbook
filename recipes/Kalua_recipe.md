# Kalua recipe

## Ingredients

## Instructions

1/2 c medium roast coffee beans

1\-1/2 t cacao nibs

1/4 vanilla bean\* (halved lengthwise)

8 oz vodka

4 oz brandy

\*I’ve never used 1/4 of bean, always a whole (go big or go home, is my motto!)

4 oz simple syrup \- this is for after the above ingredients have finished their bath 😉

1\) Place coffee beans, cacao nibs, vanilla bean, vodka, and brandy in pint\-size glass jar. Cover tightly and shake to combine. Store jar in cool, dark place for 1 week, shaking mixture once every other day.

2\) set fine mesh strainer in medium bowl; line with triple layer of cheesecloth. Strain vodka mixture through prepared strainer; discard solids

3\) Return infused strained vodka mixture to clean jar; add simple syrup

4\) Cover and gently shake to combine. Can be stored in cool dark place for up to one year.

Notes about coffee: Once I made “mistake” and used Sbux french roast, and it was smooooooky, initially. (I was gonna ditch it, but had second thoughts, I just let it continue infusing; didn’t want to waste ingredients, after all.) It turned out okay, but I’m careful to read the labels for nuances of particular beans.

For
 a more intensely flavored liqueur, you can substitute dark\-roast coffee
 beans for the medium\-roast; we do not recommend using light\-roast
beans. You can substitute 1½ teaspoons cocoa powder for the cacao nibs,
but the resulting liqueur will be slightly cloudy. You will need a
pint\-size glass jar with a tight\-fitting lid for this recipe. This
recipe can be doubled.

1\.

Place
 coffee beans, cacao nibs, vanilla bean, vodka, and brandy in pint\-size
glass jar. Cover tightly and shake to combine. Store jar in cool, dark
place for 1 week, shaking mixture once every other day.

2\.

Set
fine\-mesh strainer in medium bowl and line with triple layer of
cheesecloth. Strain vodka mixture through prepared strainer; discard
solids. Return infused vodka mixture to clean jar and add simple syrup.
Cover and gently shake to combine. (Coffee Liqueur can be stored in
cool, dark place for up to 1 year. Shake gently before using.)
