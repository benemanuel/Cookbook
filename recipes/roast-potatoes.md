# Geröstete Kartoffeln

![](/images/roast-potatoes.jpg)

## Ingredients

* **2kg** potatoes (mainly waxy)
* **60ml** olive oil
* **4 sprigs** fresh rosemary
* **6 cloves** garlic
* Pepper and salt
* **1 package** baking soda

---

## Preparation

### Preparation

* Peel the potatoes and cut them into equal-sized pieces (quarter them if they are medium-sized).
* Finely chop the rosemary and place it in a small bowl. Peel the garlic and press it through a garlic press into the same bowl as the rosemary.
* Fill a large pot with water (approx. 2L), salt the water heavily (approx. 25g salt), and add one package of baking soda. Bring the water to a boil.
* Heat a pan with the olive oil over medium heat.
* Preheat the oven to 200°C convection.

### Boiling the Potatoes

Place the potato pieces into the pot with boiling water and cook for about 10 minutes until they are soft enough to be easily cut through with a knife.

### Sautéing Rosemary and Garlic

While the potato pieces are still boiling, add the rosemary and garlic to the pan with the olive oil. As soon as the garlic starts to turn brown (approx. 2-3 minutes), pour the olive oil with the garlic and rosemary through a sieve into a small bowl.

### Roasting the Potatoes

Drain the water and let the potato pieces steam off in the pot for about 30 seconds. Then add the oil (without the garlic and rosemary), season with salt and pepper, and shake them up. It is fine if the potatoes become a bit "mushy" around the edges. Afterwards, place the potatoes on a baking tray and put them in the oven. Turn them once after 20 minutes and then leave them in the oven for another approx. 30 minutes (until they are beautifully brown).

### Seasoning the Potatoes

Take the potatoes out of the oven and transfer them back into a bowl. Now mix them with the garlic and rosemary and serve immediately.

---

## Notes

* The baking soda breaks down the pectins in the potatoes, which results in a larger surface area.
* The garlic and rosemary are only added to the potatoes after roasting in the oven, otherwise they would burn.
