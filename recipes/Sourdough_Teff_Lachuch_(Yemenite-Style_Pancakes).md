# Sourdough Teff Lachuch (Yemenite-Style Pancakes)

This recipe adapts the traditional Yemenite Lachuch technique to a teff\-based sourdough ferment. The result is a soft, spongy flatbread with a signature "honeycomb" surface, perfect for scooping up soups or spreads.

Yields: Approximately 4–6 pancakes

Hydration: \~150% (Heavy cream consistency)

## Ingredients

\* 140g Teff Flour (Brown or Ivory)

\* 125g Active Sourdough Starter (100% hydration)

\* 210ml Water (Room temperature)

\* 2g Fine Sea Salt (About ½ tsp)

\* Optional: 1 tsp Sugar (To help the yeast activity and browning)

Equipment

\* Non\-stick skillet or seasoned cast iron

\* A tight\-fitting lid

\* A ladle

## Instructions

1\. Mix and Ferment

\* In a medium bowl, whisk together the teff flour, sourdough starter, and salt.

\* Gradually whisk in the water until the batter is smooth. It should have the consistency of heavy cream—runny enough to spread itself, but thick enough to coat a spoon.

\* Cover the bowl and let it ferment at room temperature for 4 to 8 hours (or overnight in a cool spot). You want to see tiny active bubbles on the surface before cooking.

2\. Prepare the Pan

\* Place your skillet over medium heat.

\* The Secret: Do not let the pan get "searing" hot. For Lachuch, the pan should be just warm enough to start the bubbles, but not so hot that it sets the bottom immediately.

3\. The Pour

\* Ladle about 60ml to 80ml (¼ to ⅓ cup) of batter into the center of the pan.

\* Use the back of the ladle to gently spread the batter in a spiral motion toward the edges.

4\. Create the Honeycomb

\* Leave the pancake uncovered for the first minute. You will see bubbles (the "eyes") forming from the outside in.

\* Once the surface is full of holes but still looks slightly wet, place the lid on the pan.

\* Steam for 30–60 seconds until the top is set and matte. Do not flip the pancake.

5\. The Cool Down (Crucial Step)

\* Remove the pancake and place it on a clean kitchen towel (do not stack them while hot, or they will stick).

\* Before cooking the next one: Take the pan off the heat and wipe the surface with a very wet cloth or briefly run the bottom of the pan under cold water. This lowers the temperature so the next pancake develops holes properly.

Serving Suggestion

Serve warm with Hilbeh (fenugreek dip), Skhug (chili paste), or simply brushed with melted butter or honey.
