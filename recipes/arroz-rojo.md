## Arroz Rojo / Mexican Rice

## Ingredients

### For 2 Servings

* **200g** long-grain rice
* **2 tablespoons** olive oil
* **1** medium onion
* **4** cloves of garlic
* **1** large carrot
* **1** red bell pepper
* **4** tomatoes
* **1 cup** peas
* **2** red chilies
* **250ml** vegetable broth
* **1/2 teaspoon** salt
* **1/2 teaspoon** pepper
* **1/2 teaspoon** cumin
* **1 teaspoon** chili powder

---

## Preparation

### Prepare the Rice

Remove the starch from the rice. To do this, place the rice in a bowl of water and wash thoroughly. Then, pour into a sieve and repeat with fresh water until the water is no longer cloudy. This step is important, otherwise the rice can easily become mushy.

### Prepare the Ingredients

Finely chop the onion and garlic, place them in a small bowl, and set aside.

Chop the carrot, bell pepper, and chilies into small pieces and place them in a bowl. Add the peas to the same bowl. Add the spices to this bowl as well.

Puree the tomatoes.

### Sauté Onions and Garlic

Add the olive oil to a pot and heat over medium heat. Subsequently, sauté the onions and garlic for about 2 minutes.

### Toast the Rice

Now add the rice and sauté for about 15 minutes over low heat (level 3-4).
		 

### Add the Ingredients

Now add all the ingredients—except for the pureed tomatoes and the vegetable broth—into the pot and sauté for about 3 minutes.

### Cook the Rice

Pour the pureed tomatoes and the vegetable broth into the pot, bring to a brief boil, and then let it simmer for about 15 minutes over low heat until the rice is done and all the liquid has evaporated.

### Let the Rice Rest

Remove the pot from the heat and let the rice rest for about 4 minutes, fluffing it with a fork if necessary.
